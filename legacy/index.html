Curatorial Statement

The first car with an electronically controlled all-wheel-drive system, automatic tire pressure monitoring, and a 0–60 time of under 3.5 seconds. You're probably thinking this was created in the 2000s, or that it's a Lamborghini you see on the streets today. But no. This was the early 1980s, and Porsche came out with the 959.

This is just one of four moments where supercars changed the world. This virtual museum examines the four cars I believe had the greatest impact on modern automobiles: beginning with the Lamborghini Countach in the 1970s, followed by the Porsche 959 in the 1980s, then the McLaren F1 in the 1990s, and finally the Bugatti Veyron in the 2000s.

Each of these cars wasn't just fast for its time, they were exceptional. They defined the visual language and technology of today's supercars, influencing everything from production cars to motorsports. One of the challenges of a project like this is balance: making sure each decade is represented with the right analytical weight, and that the content remains precise.

Going through this exhibit, visitors will see not just a great visual representation of the beauty of these cars, but will understand the era's complexities and ambitions as expressed through them, cars that felt impossibly futuristic for their time, yet in hindsight seem inevitable.
