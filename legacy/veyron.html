Bugatti Veyron EB 16.4 Manufacturer: Bugatti Automobiles S.A.S. · Production: 2005–2011 · Country: France

The Veyron was a deliberate engineering challenge from Volkswagen Group: build the most powerful, fastest, and most technically sophisticated road car in history. The result was a 1,001 hp, 8.0-liter W16 quad-turbocharged engine in a car that could reach 253 mph while remaining fully air-conditioned, quiet, and comfortable enough for daily use. It was widely considered impossible until it existed.

1,001 Horsepower
8.0L W16 Quad-Turbo Engine
2.5s 0–60 mph
253 Top Speed (mph)
10 Radiators Required
450 Total Produced

Autocar First Drive, 2005 Primary Source · First Drive Review · Autocar Magazine · Steve Cropley

Among the first published road evaluations of the Veyron in production form. Captures the gap between press conference claims and the experience of actually driving 1,001 horsepower on public roads. The review photographs show the car before it became universally familiar, when it was still a shock.

This first-drive article captures what it was like to encounter the Veyron for the very first time, the journalist's reaction and a car that was built to break every record that existed. At 1,001 horsepower and 253 miles per hour, this was something never seen before in a production car. Bugatti had to specially engineer tires with Michelin and brakes with Brembo that could survive sustained speeds above 250 mph, with the tires rated for only 15 minutes at full speed before needing replacement. The fact that in the 2000s you could go and actually buy one of these is remarkable. It closed out the era by becoming the benchmark for everything, and since then everyone has been trying to beat it.
