Porsche 959

Before the future arrived, Porsche built it.

Porsche 959 Manufacturer: Porsche AG · Production: 1986–1993 · Country: West Germany

The 959 was originally developed as a racing homologation special for Group B rallying and endurance racing. It became the most technologically advanced road car of its era — featuring electronically managed all-wheel drive, adaptive suspension, hollow-spoke magnesium wheels, and the world's first production tire pressure monitoring system.

The Technology Gap

To understand the 959's significance, consider what production cars looked like in 1986. Front-wheel drive was cutting-edge for mass market vehicles. Electronic driver aids were science fiction. ABS was a luxury option on sedans.

The 959 arrived with electronically managed all-wheel drive that could distribute torque between axles in real time — a system so sophisticated that Porsche used lessons from it in every subsequent generation of 911. It also featured electronically adjustable dampers, six-speed gearbox, and a twin-turbocharged flat-six with sequential boost delivery.

Kimble's cutaway analysis for Motorsport.com reveals the depth of engineering involved: even the wheel choices — hollow magnesium spokes — were made with weight and heat dissipation in mind at a level no other manufacturer was attempting.

Legacy in Technology

The 959 was the first production car to include tire pressure monitoring sensors — a technology that is now federally mandated in every car sold in the United States. What Porsche offered as a supercar exclusive in 1986 is now a legal requirement.

The Car and Driver test from 1987 is revealing not just for its numbers but for its language of disbelief. American automotive journalists, accustomed to muscle cars and European GT cars, struggled to contextualize what they were experiencing: a car that handled with the precision of a racing machine while remaining genuinely livable.

The 959's AWD architecture directly influenced the Porsche Carrera 4, which debuted in 1989 and brought that technology to the masses. Today, nearly every Porsche offered uses a version of that system.
